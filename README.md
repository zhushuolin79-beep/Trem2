Spatiotemporal Transcriptomics Reveals Macrophage-Dependent Niches Driving Angiogenesis in Ischemic Cardiac Repair

Abstract
Objective: Myocardial ischemia-reperfusion (IR) injury is a critical determinant of post-infarction heart failure. Therefore, systematic elucidation of IR injury repair mechanisms is essential for ameliorating adverse cardiac outcomes.
Methods and Results: By integrating multi-timepoint spatial transcriptomics with single-cell RNA-seq, we delineated a panoramic atlas of repair-associated cell types following IR injury. Trem2⁺ macrophage, peaking at 72 hours, function as a regulatory hub in the ischemic and dangerous zone, mediated cardiac repair and exhibited conserved transcriptional signatures across ischemic organs. Using the Trem2 Cre/DTR system, specific ablation of Trem2+ macrophage significantly impaired cardiac reparative capacity, as evidenced by decreased neovascularization, worsened cardiac function, and exacerbated fibrosis. Conversely, adoptive transfer of Trem2⁺ macrophages efficiently promoted cardiac repair. Spatiotemporal interaction analysis and co culture experiments identified Jag1-Notch1 as a key mediator of Trem2⁺ macrophage-endothelial cell crosstalk. Specific deletion of Jag1 in Trem2+ macrophages attenuated their pro-angiogenic activity. Through genetic deletion of Trem2, pharmacological agonism, and endogenous ligand inhibition, we further showed that the Trem2 SYK signaling axis controls Jag1 expression and macrophage pro angiogenic function.
Conclusion: Trem2+ macrophages are essential for post ischemic myocardial recovery, suggesting that Trem2 Jag1 signaling is a potential therapeutic target for cardiac repair.

Key words: Spatiotemporal transcriptomics; Ischemic cardiac repair; Macrophage-endothelial cell niche; Trem2 signaling
